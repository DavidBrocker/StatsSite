Catalog Course Description:

This course will introduce students to the basic descriptive and inferential statistics used in the behavioral and social sciences. Topics will include the organization of data, measures of central tendency and variability, correlation and regression, hypothesis testing, and various parametric and nonparametric tests of significance including t-tests and ANOVA. Students will focus on the interconnections between theory, statistical techniques, and research methods in order to identify the appropriate statistical tests to analyze data and reach objective conclusions regarding research questions in the social sciences. The course will provide practice in using statistical software for data summarization, presentation, and analysis.

Course Learning Outcomes:

  • As a result of this course, students will be able to understand statistical terms and research reports in the social sciences, use statistical techniques to analyze experimental data for hypothesis testing, and use statistical software to aid in the presentation and analysis of quantitative data.

  • To accomplish these objectives, students will be able to:

    • Describe the four measurement scales (nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio).

    • Construct frequency distributions and represent them graphically.

    • Identify and calculate measures of central tendency including mean, median, and mode.

    • Define and calculate measures of variability including variance and standard deviation.

    • Calculate and apply the use of z-scores.

    • Describe the normal curve and use the curve to solve various problems including probability.

    • Appraise the difference between populations and samples.

    • Apply the concepts of sampling theory and sampling distributions to statistical testing.

    • Explain and apply the basic concepts of hypothesis testing including the null, significance testing, and errors.

    • Calculate and appraise correlation coefficients.

    • Obtain and graph regression equations and use results to make predictions.

    • Test for significant differences between means using t-tests.

    • Calculate and interpret analysis of variance and follow up comparisons.

    • Determine appropriate hypothesis test based on research questions and level of data.

    • Evaluate how appropriate measures are by measuring reliability and validity.

    • Perform statistical techniques by hand using formulas and with statistical software.

    • Interpret the results of inferential statistical techniques.

Course Format

This course has both lecture and laboratory components. During lecture on Monday and Wednesday, we will discuss statistical theory and prepare for the corresponding laboratory by examining computational formulas. We will also work through practice examples to prepare you for the recitation.

On Monday afternoons we will meet for recitation. I will guide you through the first half of the lab for that day, and students will be given the remainder of the recitation period to complete the second portion of the lab. Students will complete the labs using statistical software accessed on the computers in our classroom.

Grading:

Exams (36%) – 12 points each

There will be three non-cumulative exams. Each of the exams will contain two parts: conceptional and computational. The conceptual part of the exam will include 12 multiple choice questions pertaining to theory we covered in lecture. Each multiple choice question will be worth 0.5 points for a total of 6 points. The computational part of the exam will contain 2 to 3 questions with various parts, each worth a different point value. The computational portion will require you to apply the concepts we learned in lecture and the skills we learn in recitation.

Labs (54%) – 4.5 points each

In recitation each week, students will complete a 5-point lab assignment that corresponds to the lectures given the week before. Each lab will contain two parts. We will complete the first part together as a class. Working along with the class to complete the first part is worth 1.5 points. The second part is to be completed by students on their own or in small groups and is worth the remaining 3 points.

Attendance and Participation (10%)

This course requires attendance and active participation, as the material increases in difficulty over the course of the semester. Each student is allowed 2 absences without penalty, so long as missed work is made up in a timely manner. Students who are absence more than twice, should meet with me to discuss how they will keep up with the course material.
